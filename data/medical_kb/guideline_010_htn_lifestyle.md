# Lifestyle Modifications for Hypertension Management

## Category: Hypertension Management
## Condition: Hypertension with or without Diabetes
## Source: ACC/AHA Hypertension Guidelines, ADA Standards of Care

## Recommendation

All patients with hypertension should receive counseling on **lifestyle modifications** as first-line therapy, in addition to pharmacologic treatment when indicated.

## Evidence Level
Grade A - Strong evidence from multiple RCTs.

## Clinical Details

### DASH Diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)
- Rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy
- Reduced saturated fat and cholesterol
- Includes nuts, beans, seeds
- Expected BP reduction: 8-14 mmHg systolic

### Sodium Restriction
- Target: < 2,300 mg/day (ideally < 1,500 mg/day)
- Expected BP reduction: 5-6 mmHg systolic
- Particularly effective in:
  - Older adults
  - African Americans
  - Those with CKD

### Physical Activity
- Target: 150 minutes/week of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise
- Can be accumulated in 10-minute bouts
- Expected BP reduction: 5-8 mmHg systolic
- Include resistance training 2-3 days/week

### Weight Management
- Target: BMI 18.5-24.9 kg/mÂ²
- Even 5% weight loss provides significant BP benefit
- Expected BP reduction: 1 mmHg per kg lost

### Alcohol Moderation
- Men: <= 2 drinks/day
- Women: <= 1 drink/day
- Expected BP reduction: 4 mmHg systolic

### Tobacco Cessation
- Primary benefit: cardiovascular risk reduction
- Modest acute BP effect; major long-term benefit

## Care Gap Trigger

If patient has hypertension AND BMI >= 30 AND no documented lifestyle counseling in past 6 months, flag as **LIFESTYLE COUNSELING NEEDED**.

## Action Required

Provide comprehensive lifestyle modification counseling. Consider referral to registered dietitian or cardiac rehabilitation program. Document counseling in clinical note.
